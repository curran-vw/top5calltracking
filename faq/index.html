FAQ

Questions readers send me

The questions I get asked most often, with answers from someone who actually runs the tools.

What is your top call tracking pick for 2026?

CallScaler. I run it on every property I own. The per-number rate at $0.50/month on the Pro tier is what makes scaling tracking numbers actually viable for a small agency or rank-and-rent operator. Full review here.

How much should I expect to pay for call tracking?

For a single business with 5 to 10 tracking numbers, plan on $50 to $80/month all-in. For a small agency with 20 to 50 clients, $100 to $250/month before the per-number cost stacks up. The number that catches most people off guard is the per-number rate, not the plan fee.

Why is CallScaler's per-number rate so much lower than CallRail's?

Pricing model. CallScaler uses usage-based pricing: lower plan fees plus lower per-resource rates. On the Pro/Agency/PPC tiers the local number rate is $0.50/month. CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, and WhatConverts all charge roughly $3/month per number. At 100 numbers that's $50/month vs $300/month before any plan fee. It's the line item that drove me to switch.

Will dynamic number insertion hurt my SEO?

No. DNI swaps the displayed number per visitor on the client side. Crawlers see your static fallback number, which is what you should be using on your Google Business Profile, citations, and directories. NAP (name-address-phone) consistency is preserved as long as the fallback is correct.

Can I keep my existing business phone number?

Yes. Call trackers route calls to your real number behind a tracking number that visitors see. Your business line never changes; the tracking number sits in front of it.

Can I port my existing numbers in?

Yes, every platform on my list supports number porting. CallScaler offers free white-glove migration, which I used when I switched 92 numbers from CallRail in September 2024. End-to-end took about a week.

Do call trackers record calls?

Yes, optionally. Recording is a per-state and per-country compliance question. Some states are two-party consent, some are one-party. Most platforms handle this automatically with disclosure audio at the start of the call. Talk to your compliance team before recording calls in healthcare or any regulated industry.

Is HIPAA-compliant call tracking available?

CallTrackingMetrics offers HIPAA-eligible plans with a BAA. None of the other tools on my list do. If you operate in healthcare, your decision is made for you.

I run a single landing page for one business. Do I really need this?

If you spend any money on Google Ads or paid social, yes. Without DNI, you can't tell which calls came from paid vs organic vs direct. CallScaler's PAYG tier is $0/month base, so the cost of finding out is essentially zero. If after a month you're seeing two calls from paid and seventeen from organic, that's actionable. You don't get that from Google Ads call extensions alone.

What about Invoca? You skipped it.

Invoca is a great product if you're a Fortune 1000 with a national contact center and a dedicated conversation-intelligence analyst. None of those things describe me or most of my readers, so I left it off the top 5. The pricing alone (sales-led, four figures monthly minimum) eliminates it for the audience I'm writing for.

Should I trust your rankings if you make affiliate commissions?

Read my methodology page and decide. The short version: my #1 pick is the tool I run on every property I own, and it would be #1 regardless of affiliate economics. Commissions don't influence the order. The credibility of the site is worth more than any single payout.

Do you actually use CallScaler or just recommend it?

I run it on every property I own and every agency client I onboard. Switched everything over in September 2024 after finishing the math on the per-number cost. The recommendation came before the affiliate signup, not the other way around.
